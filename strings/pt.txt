HELP_REQUIREMENTS_TITLE Browser & Device Requirements
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_DECK What you need to use PayCal: a modern browser, a secure HTTPS connection, and a device that supports passkeys.
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_WHY_TITLE Why These Requirements Exist
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_WHY_TEXT PayCal encrypts your data with a key that is unlocked by your passkey. Without a WebAuthn-capable device the encryption chain cannot be completed. These requirements reflect the cryptographic model that keeps your data private.
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_WEBAUTHN_TITLE WebAuthn Support Required
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_WEBAUTHN_TEXT PayCal uses the Web Authentication API (WebAuthn / FIDO2) for passkey registration and login. Browsers without WebAuthn support cannot complete registration or encrypted-data unlock. This is intentional and part of the security design.
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_HTTPS_TITLE HTTPS and Secure Context
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_HTTPS_TEXT WebAuthn only operates over a secure HTTPS connection. Attempts to register or authenticate over plain HTTP will be blocked by your browser. This protects the integrity of the passkey exchange and prevents credential interception.
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_PASSKEYS_TITLE Passkey-Capable Device or Platform Authenticator
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_PASSKEYS_TEXT Your device must be able to create and verify a passkey. This is typically a built-in biometric sensor or device PIN, referred to as a platform authenticator.
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_PASSKEYS_ITEMS_TITLE Supported authenticator types
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_PASSKEYS_ITEM_1 Built-in biometric: Touch ID, Face ID, Windows Hello, or Android biometric prompt
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_PASSKEYS_ITEM_2 Device PIN as a fallback when biometrics are unavailable
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_PASSKEYS_ITEM_3 Roaming authenticator: a hardware security key such as a FIDO2-compatible YubiKey
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_BROWSERS_TITLE Supported Browsers
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_BROWSERS_TEXT Use a modern, actively maintained browser. The following are known to work with WebAuthn passkeys:
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_BROWSER_1 Chrome 100 or later (desktop and Android)
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_BROWSER_2 Safari 16 or later (macOS 13+ and iOS 16+)
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_BROWSER_3 Firefox 119 or later (desktop)
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_BROWSER_4 Edge 100 or later
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_BROWSER_5 Brave (based on a recent Chromium release)
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_BROWSER_CAVEAT Older browser versions, non-mainstream forks, and browsers with WebAuthn APIs disabled do not meet the minimum requirement.
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_UNSUPPORTED_TITLE If You See an Unsupported Browser Warning
HELP_REQUIREMENTS_UNSUPPORTED_TEXT Update your browser, switch to a supported browser, or use a device that meets the requirements above. PayCal will not silently downgrade to a less secure path.
HELP_WEBAUTHN_TITLE WebAuthn Security and Privacy Controls
HELP_WEBAUTHN_DECK How WebAuthn, passkeys, and PayCal encryption work together to keep your data private.
HELP_WEBAUTHN_WHY_TITLE Why PayCal Requires WebAuthn
HELP_WEBAUTHN_WHY_TEXT PayCal uses passkeys for account registration and secure unlock. If your browser does not support WebAuthn you cannot complete passkey registration or unlock encrypted entries. This is intentional and part of the security design.
HELP_WEBAUTHN_KEK_DEK_TITLE The KEK and DEK Encryption Model
HELP_WEBAUTHN_KEK_DEK_TEXT PayCal protects your encrypted data with two keys. The DEK locks and unlocks entry data. The KEK protects the DEK when stored. Think of it as a key inside a lockbox.
HELP_WEBAUTHN_DEK_LABEL DEK (Data Encryption Key)
HELP_WEBAUTHN_DEK_DESC The key that locks and unlocks your entry data.
HELP_WEBAUTHN_KEK_LABEL KEK (Key Encryption Key)
HELP_WEBAUTHN_KEK_DESC A second key that protects the DEK when stored.
HELP_WEBAUTHN_PRIVACY_TITLE What This Means for Privacy
HELP_WEBAUTHN_PRIVACY_TEXT The privacy goal is simple: reduce exposure of sensitive keys and keep encrypted data protected at rest.
HELP_WEBAUTHN_PRIVACY_ITEM_1 The DEK is used only when needed for active encrypt or decrypt operations.
HELP_WEBAUTHN_PRIVACY_ITEM_2 Stored DEK material remains wrapped, not saved as plaintext key bytes.
HELP_WEBAUTHN_PRIVACY_ITEM_3 Passkey-based unlock avoids relying on reusable password secrets for primary decrypt flows.
HELP_WEBAUTHN_PRIVACY_ITEM_4 Unsupported browsers are blocked from passkey-required paths instead of being silently downgraded.
HELP_WEBAUTHN_UNSUPPORTED_TITLE If You See an Unsupported Browser Warning
HELP_WEBAUTHN_UNSUPPORTED_TEXT Use a modern browser with WebAuthn support over HTTPS then retry registration or unlock.
